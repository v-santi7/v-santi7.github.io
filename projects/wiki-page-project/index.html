Crown shyness

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

For the album by Trash Boat, see Crown Shyness (album).

Crown shyness (also canopy disengagement,[1] canopy shyness,[2] or inter-crown spacing[3] ) is a phenomenon observed in some tree species, in which the crowns of fully stocked trees do not touch each other, forming a canopy with channel-like gaps.[4][5]The phenomenon is most prevalent among trees of the same species, but also occurs between trees of different species.
